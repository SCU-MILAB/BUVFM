## Abstract

Breast cancer is the most common cancer among women worldwide, and early detection through effective screening can significantly improve clinical outcomes. 
Breast cancer ultrasound screening is particularly valuable for women with dense breasts, which are common in Asian populations, yet the reliance on physician-operated acquisition and interpretation largely limits its scalability, especially in underserved regions.
Here we present a cloud-edge collaborative Artificial Intelligence-driven Breast cancer Ultrasound Screening (AIBUS) system with portable devices for decentralized and standardized screening in rural settings.
It integrates an edge-deployed real-time scanning quality control module that provides operator-facing acquisition guidance, along with a cloud-side risk stratification module for consistent interpretation.
These AI components were developed from a self-supervised breast ultrasound video foundation model (BUVFM) built in this study, which is pre-trained on 84,021 ultrasound video sequences and 423,493 separate frames from real-world breast cancer ultrasound screening.
In a volunteer scanning study, AI-assisted non-physician operators achieved scanning performance non-inferior to that of experienced ultrasound physicians.
In retrospective validation, the cloud-based module consistently outperformed representative deep-learning models, achieving a macro-sensitivity of 95.42\% and macro-specificity of 95.60\% in the internal validation cohort, and demonstrating robust generalization across 11 heterogeneous external cohorts with macro-sensitivities ranging from 94.11\% to 99.39\% and macro-specificities ranging from 94.28\% to 99.39\%.
In a video-based reader study involving 12 physicians experienced in breast ultrasound interpretation, standalone AIBUS outperformed all unaided readers, while AI assistance improved readers' macro-sensitivity and macro-specificity by 6.79\% and 1.96\%, respectively, and reduced interpretation time by 29.68\%.
In the prospective study conducted across 11 screening sites ($n=3,528$), AIBUS completed examinations in an average time of nearly two minutes and maintained an overall macro-sensitivity of 90.95\% and macro-specificity of 96.90\%.
These findings suggest that AIBUS can significantly reduce dependence on specialist hospitals and experienced ultrasound physicians, and establish a new paradigm for population-scale, high-quality screening. This system is promising for nationwide large-scale breast cancer screening in rural settings.

## The Architecture of AIBUS

AIBUS is a cloud–edge collaborative system designed to support standardized breast ultrasound video acquisition and BI-RADS-based risk stratification using portable ultrasound devices. The system consists of an edge-side quality control module and a cloud-side BI-RADS-based risk stratification module. Both are built upon the breast ultrasound video foundation model (BUVFM) developed in this study, which serves as the backbone of the cloud-side module and as the teacher model for distilling spatiotemporal knowledge into lightweight edge-side models.

### <SmallCaps>1. Edge-Side Quality Control Module</SmallCaps>

Running on portable ultrasound devices, this module performs two complementary functions in real time during scanning:

**Acquisition Guidance Component.** Provides real-time operator-facing feedback on four image quality factors — probe contact, imaging depth, scanning speed, and gain setting — plus scan completeness via a trajectory display:

- **Probe contact:** Evaluated by adaptive greyscale histogram analysis to detect acoustic shadow artefacts from incomplete skin coupling.
- **Imaging depth:** Assessed by a lightweight semantic segmentation model that generates real-time binary masks of the glandular region and pectoralis major muscle to identify insufficient depth.
- **Scanning speed:** Monitored via inter-frame similarity from hashing-based representations and Hamming distances; abrupt drops indicate excessive transducer motion.
- **Gain setting:** Obtained from the hardware interface and compared against a predefined acceptable range.
- **Scan completeness:** A real-time trajectory display visualizes probe movement over a standardized breast surface model (constructed in Blender), guiding a quadrant-by-quadrant sweep pattern. Trajectory updates are gated by ultrasound image similarity to suppress false displacement from stationary probe tilting.

These signals are converted into visual prompts and warnings, allowing non-physician operators to dynamically adjust acquisition parameters for standardized whole-breast scanning.

**Representative Clip Selection Component.** Operates in parallel to retain informative clips for cloud analysis while reducing redundant data transfer. It uses a two-stage workflow:

1. **Frame validity assessment:** MobileNetV3 classifies each incoming frame as interpretable or invalid; invalid frames are discarded.
2. **Lesion candidate detection:** A YOLO11-based detector localizes suspicious findings in valid frames and produces confidence scores.

Both models were optimized via task-specific supervision and feature-level teacher–student distillation, with the pretrained BUVFM serving as the teacher. When lesion detection persists for eight consecutive frames, a lesion-positive 60-frame (3-second) clip is saved; if no lesion is detected for eight consecutive seconds, a lesion-negative clip is saved. For each examination, up to six clips are uploaded to the cloud — three from each breast, selected by highest lesion-presence confidence.

### <SmallCaps>2. Cloud-Side BI-RADS Risk Stratification Module</SmallCaps>

The cloud-side module processes representative clips uploaded from the edge. It is initialized from the pretrained BUVFM backbone and fine-tuned end-to-end with an attention-based pooling and classification head. For each input clip, the video encoder generates spatiotemporal embeddings, which are aggregated by attentive pooling into a compact clip-level representation and mapped by a linear output layer to **Low**, **Medium**, or **High** risk categories. During fine-tuning, static ultrasound images are converted into pseudo-video clips by temporal replication, enabling both static and dynamic data to be used within the same video backbone. The highest predicted risk category across all clips from a participant's examination is taken as the participant-level result.

### <SmallCaps>3. Breast Ultrasound Video Foundation Model (BUVFM)</SmallCaps>

BUVFM was pretrained on 84,021 unlabelled dynamic video sequences (4,957,239 frames) and 423,493 static ultrasound frames via a self-supervised objective that captures spatiotemporal consistency across probe sweeps and imaging planes. Rather than reconstructing raw pixels — which can overemphasize speckle noise and acoustic shadowing — BUVFM adopts the Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA), which performs prediction in latent representation space. To better model the continuous latent changes induced by manual probe sweeps, we introduced the neural memory ordinary differential equation (nmODE) into the JEPA predictor, reformulating the activation process as a continuous dynamical system. Static frames are converted into pseudo-video clips through temporal replication so that both modalities share a common video backbone. The resulting representations are stable, noise-tolerant, and capture the dynamic nature of breast ultrasound scanning.

## Experimental Results

### Retrospective Validation

AIBUS-Cloud was evaluated on one internal validation cohort ($n=1{,}170$) and 11 external validation cohorts ($n=10{,}855$ total) against representative state-of-the-art models, including OpenUS, USFM, EchoCare, UltraFedFM (ultrasound foundation models), VideoMAE v2, VideoVAE+ (video self-supervised methods), and USCL (convolutional baseline). All models were fine-tuned under the same BI-RADS-based three-class risk stratification setting.



| **Internal Validation** | Accuracy | Balanced ACC | Macro-Sens. | Macro-Spec. | Macro-AUC |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **AIBUS-Cloud (Ours)** | **90.17** | **95.42** | **95.42** | **95.60** | **0.9849** |
| VideoMAE v2 | 77.52 | 84.23 | 84.23 | 89.35 | 0.9378 |
| EchoCare | 71.79 | 74.70 | 74.70 | 85.34 | 0.8342 |
| OpenUS | 66.41 | 70.29 | 70.29 | 82.43 | 0.8404 |
| VideoVAE+ | 71.20 | 76.59 | 76.59 | 85.93 | 0.8568 |
| USFM | 61.88 | 62.16 | 62.16 | 80.97 | 0.7784 |
| USCL | 62.65 | 63.27 | 63.27 | 81.79 | 0.8145 |
| UltraFedFM | 66.07 | 60.75 | 60.75 | 80.98 | 0.7914 |


AIBUS-Cloud consistently outperformed all compared methods across all metrics ($P<0.001$ for all pairwise comparisons). Across the 11 external validation cohorts, AIBUS-Cloud maintained robust generalization with macro-AUC ranging from **0.9800 to 0.9999**, and achieved an aggregated accuracy of **95.04%** and macro-AUC of **0.9958** on the combined external set ($n=10{,}855$).

### Reader Study

Twelve ultrasound physicians evaluated 564 breast ultrasound video cases in a crossover design with a 4-week washout. Readers were stratified by cumulative breast ultrasound reading volume into junior ($\leq5{,}000$ cases) and senior ($>5{,}000$ cases) groups.

|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Junior — Unaided | 74.16 | 92.00 | 75.28 | 35.2 | — |
| **Junior — AI-Assisted** | **82.40$^{**}$** | **94.47$^{*}$** | **80.23$^{**}$** | **26.9$^{*}$** | — |
| Senior — Unaided | 78.06 | 93.37 | 77.30 | 25.1 | — |
| **Senior — AI-Assisted** | **82.85$^{*}$** | **94.61$^{*}$** | **80.20$^{\,ns}$** | **14.5$^{**}$** | — |

### Prospective Real-World Deployment

AIBUS was prospectively deployed at 11 screening sites across Sichuan and Gansu provinces ($n=3{,}528$ participants, 21,168 breast ultrasound videos). Operators used portable devices with real-time AI guidance; representative clips were automatically selected on the edge and analysed by the cloud-side module.



| **Prospective Deployment** | Participants | Accuracy (%) | Macro-Sens. (%) | Macro-Spec. (%) | Macro-AUC | Mean Screening Time |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Overall** | 3,528 | **97.51** | **90.95** | **96.90** | **0.9913** | 1 min 55 s |
| Sichuan | 2,723 | 97.94 | 92.05 | 96.35 | — | — |
| Gansu | 805 | 96.02 | 86.14 | 97.60 | — | — |

No significant regional differences were observed in macro-sensitivity, macro-specificity, or macro-precision (all $P>0.05$). Performance remained stable across age groups, healthcare levels (city, town, village), and operators (all $P>0.05$ for sensitivity and specificity). The mean screening time of 1 min 55 s per participant supports high-throughput deployment in resource-constrained settings.
