## Abstract

**Breast cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer among women**, for which **early detection is critical to improving outcomes**. Unlike popularly used mammography for breast cancer screening in western countries, **ultrasound is the primary screening modality in Asian populations** due to the high prevalence of dense breast tissue. However, the **conventional doctor- and hospital-dependent screening paradigm is impossible for large scale screening**, particularly in remote and underserved regions.

Here, we present an **artificial intelligence–enabled system for large-scale breast cancer ultrasound screening (AIBS)**. **AIBS establishes a doctor-independent scanning workflow** by integrating **real-time quality control into the acquisition process**, which enables **non-expert operators** to perform standardized examinations and consistently capture diagnostically adequate videos. The system further adopts an **edge–cloud collaboration scheme** for effective and efficient screening, where **on-device inference supports rapid lesion detection** during scanning and **cloud-based analysis provides reliable BI-RADS assessment** from uploaded video sequences. Coupled with portable ultrasound devices, the system delivers a **hospital-independent screening paradigm**, expanding accessibility to **population-level screening**.

In retrospective validation, AIBS achieved **97.44%** risk stratification classification accuracy. Clinical evaluation of the scanning quality control module showed **operator performance comparable with trained ultrasound physicians** (7.70 vs 8.17, 95% CI lower bound -0.61, *P < 0.05 for non-inferiority*). In a video-based reader study involving 16 ultrasound physicians, the system **matched or exceeded senior-expert performance** (**91.7% specificity**) and **improved diagnostic efficiency by 42%** (*P < 0.001*). In a nationwide study across 24 remote sites (n=11,485), **average screening time per case was reduced to 205.2 seconds**, with an **overall patient-level diagnostic accuracy of 91.3%**. 

These results demonstrate that **AIBS holds great promise for large-scale breast cancer screening**.

## The Three-Stage Architecture


### <SmallCaps>Three-Stage Workflow</SmallCaps>
AIBS reconciles real-time responsiveness with high-precision diagnostic requirements through a hierarchical pipeline:

### **Stage 1: Edge-Side Rapid Filtering (nmODE)**
- **Device:** Portable Ultrasound / Laptop.
- **Function:** A lightweight Neural Ordinary Differential Equation (nmODE) model continuously sieves raw video streams to detect suspicious content with low latency.

### **Stage 2: Precision Object Detection (YOLO11)**
- **Function:** Triggered by Stage 1, this model regresses bounding boxes around lesions to extract key diagnostic clips, significantly reducing the bandwidth required for cloud upload.

### **Stage 3: Cloud-Side Risk Stratification (nmODE-VJEPA)**
- **The Core Model:** A ViT-Giant Foundation Model pre-trained using the **Video Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (V-JEPA)**.
- **Temporal Modeling:** Integrates **Neural Memory ODEs** to handle irregularly sampled frames and capture continuous pathological kinematics.
- **Output:** Tripartite risk stratification (**Low, Medium, High Risk**) mapped to BI-RADS standards.

The system adopts an **Edge-Cloud collaboration**, ensuring real-time response at the front-end and expert-level analysis at the back-end.

## Experimental Results

### **Quantitative Performance**
nmODE-VJEPA outperforms SOTA models (OpenUS, USFM) across all cohorts:

- **Internal Validation (N=790):** **98.23% Accuracy**, 0.9956 Macro AUC.
- **External Validation (N=1,082):** Accuracy up to **100%** (Ext1) and **96.15%** (Ext2).
- **Prospective Real-world (N=11,485):** **91.3% Patient-level Accuracy**; No high-risk case was downgraded to low-risk.

### **Clinical Utility & Efficiency**
- **Non-Inferiority:** AI-assisted non-physicians achieved image quality scores comparable to professional sonographers (8.00 vs 8.16).
- **Efficiency:** Reading efficiency improved by **42%** ($P < 0.001$).
- **Inter-reader Agreement:** Pairwise Cohen’s kappa increased from **0.60 to 0.70** with AI assistance.
